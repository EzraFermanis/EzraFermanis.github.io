What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not me" section?

A great section. I do need to remember this section, because it not only affects the way I see myself, but also the way I see others. For example, I know someone who is always angry, so that's who I pereive them as a person, when really, I should be just saying to myself that he/she is only 'feeling' angry. He is not an angry person. The section teaches us that emotions are a state of mind, and are physiological feelings that can be controlled, like pain etc. When we look at it this way it's much easier to maintain and control our emotions, instead of letting our emotions control us.
